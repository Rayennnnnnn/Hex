About Tunisia

Tunisia is a stunning North African country known for its Mediterranean beaches, rich history, Roman ruins, and charming desert landscapes. From the blue-and-white village of Sidi Bou Said to the ancient city of Carthage, Tunisia is a bridge between Africa, Europe, and the Arab world.

Famous Landmarks

Sidi Bou Said

A peaceful coastal village painted in blue and white, overlooking the Mediterranean Sea.

El Jem Amphitheatre

A massive Roman colosseum — one of the best-preserved in the world.

Bardo Museum

One of Africa’s most important museums, famous for its Roman mosaics.

Carthage

The ancient city of Carthage — once a powerful Phoenician center of trade and culture.

Great Mosque of Kairouan

One of the oldest mosques in the Islamic world, located in the holy city of Kairouan.
